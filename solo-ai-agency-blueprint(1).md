**Target:** $30,000–$50,000 monthly revenue | Zero employees | High margin | Low overhead

## 1️⃣ Agency Positioning

### Problem Solved
**Inbound lead response delay kills revenue for service businesses.**

Most SMBs lose 50%+ of inbound leads because they respond too slowly (>5 minutes). AI-powered instant response and qualification systems capture leads 24/7, qualify them, book appointments, and hand off only sales-ready prospects.

### Recommended Niche: Home Services + Professional Services
**Primary verticals:**
- HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical contractors
- Real estate brokerages and teams
- Med spas, dental practices, cosmetic clinics
- Law firms (personal injury, family law, immigration)

### Why This Niche Buys Fast
1. **Direct revenue attribution** — Every missed call is a lost job worth $2,000–$50,000
2. **Pain is urgent** — They're bleeding leads RIGHT NOW
3. **Owner-operated decisions** — No procurement committees
4. **Marketing spend already exists** — They spend $5–30K/month on ads, losing leads at the response stage
5. **Low technical sophistication** — They won't second-guess your implementation

### Why This Is Manageable Solo
- Standard intake workflows across clients (name, service needed, availability, urgency)
- No custom ML models required — LLM + automation handles 90%
- Clients don't need enterprise SLAs
- Similar CRMs across verticals (ServiceTitan, HouseCall Pro, Clio, Follow Up Boss)
- WhatsApp/SMS-first communication fits templated workflows

### Solo Founder Competitive Advantage
- **Speed:** You deploy in 2 weeks. Agencies take 8–12 weeks.
- **Direct access:** Owner talks to the builder, not an account manager
- **Aligned incentives:** You eat what you kill — no bloated overhead
- **Technical depth:** You understand the AI, not just reselling white-label garbage
- **Flexibility:** You can customize within your template; agencies can't

## 3️⃣ Core Offer: AI Lead Response & Qualification System

### Flagship Service Definition
**"AI-Powered 24/7 Lead Capture & Qualification System"**

You install an AI agent that:
1. Responds to all inbound leads within 60 seconds (web forms, missed calls, SMS, WhatsApp)
2. Qualifies leads using client-specific criteria
3. Books appointments directly into their calendar/CRM
4. Hands off hot leads to sales team with full context
5. Nurtures cold leads automatically

### Clear Deliverables
| Phase | Deliverable | Timeline |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Week 1 | Discovery + workflow mapping | Days 1–3 |
| Week 1 | AI agent configuration + knowledge base setup | Days 4–7 |
| Week 2 | Integration with CRM/calendar/communication channels | Days 8–10 |
| Week 2 | Testing + refinement + staff training | Days 11–14 |
| Ongoing | Monitoring, optimization, monthly reporting | Monthly |

### Clear Exclusions (Scope Protection)
- ❌ Advertising management or media buying
- ❌ Website development or redesign
- ❌ CRM migration or cleanup
- ❌ Custom mobile apps
- ❌ Voice cloning or custom voice models
- ❌ Integration with unsupported legacy systems
- ❌ Unlimited revisions (3 revision rounds included)

### Implementation Timeline
- **Setup:** 10–14 business days
- **Optimization period:** First 30 days post-launch
- **Steady state:** Day 31+

### Expected Client ROI
| Metric | Before AI | After AI |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Lead response time | 2–24 hours | <60 seconds |
| After-hours lead capture | 0% | 100% |
| Lead-to-appointment rate | 15–25% | 40–60% |
| Staff time on qualification | 20+ hrs/week | 3–5 hrs/week |
| Cost per appointment | $150–$300 | $50–$100 |

**ROI Example:** Client spends $15K/month on ads, generating 300 leads. Before: 45 appointments (15%). After: 150 appointments (50%). At $5K average job value, that's $525K additional pipeline per month. Your fee is <1% of value created.

### Why It Justifies Premium Pricing
- **Immediate revenue impact** — Not a "nice to have"
- **Replaces headcount** — $4K/month for AI vs. $5K+/month for an employee
- **24/7 coverage** — No sick days, no turnover
- **Speed advantage** — First responder wins 78% of jobs

### Templatization Strategy
Build 4 vertical templates:
1. **Home Services Template** — ServiceTitan/HouseCall Pro integrations, job type qualification
2. **Real Estate Template** — Follow Up Boss/KVCore integrations, buyer/seller qualification
3. **Med Spa/Dental Template** — Appointment booking, service interest qualification
4. **Legal Template** — Case type qualification, conflict checking, consultation booking

Each new client is a template clone + customization. Target: 80% reuse, 20% customization.

| Scale | Monthly Cost |
|-------|--------------|
| 0–3 clients | $200–$350 |
| 4–6 clients | $350–$600 |
| 7–10 clients | $600–$1,000 |

### Cost Control Strategy
1. **Cache common responses** — Reduce LLM calls by 30–50%
2. **Use Claude Haiku for simple routing** — 10x cheaper than Sonnet
3. **Set per-client API budgets** — Hard limits prevent runaway costs
4. **Batch non-urgent operations** — Process overnight where possible
5. **Monitor token usage weekly** — Dashboard alerts at 80% budget

### What NOT to Use
- ❌ Kubernetes (overkill, ops burden)
- ❌ Self-hosted LLMs (cost, complexity, quality)
- ❌ Custom ML models (maintenance nightmare)
- ❌ AWS/GCP raw services (complexity)
- ❌ No-code AI builders (vendor lock-in, limited flexibility)
- ❌ Enterprise iPaaS (Workato, Tray.io — too expensive)

### Memory Handling
| Memory Type | Implementation | Retention |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Conversation memory** | PostgreSQL (Supabase) | 90 days |
| **Lead context** | Passed in each LLM call | Per session |
| **Client knowledge base** | Vector DB (pgvector) | Permanent |
| **Cross-conversation memory** | CRM lookup on lead match | Permanent |

**Memory Strategy:**
- Short-term: Last 10 messages in context window
- Long-term: Summarize conversations >10 messages, store summary
- Lead history: Pull from CRM if returning lead detected

### RAG Implementation
**Use Case:** FAQ handling, service information, pricing guidance

**Architecture:**
```
Client Onboarding → Ingest FAQs, Services, Pricing → Chunk (500 tokens) 
→ Embed (text-embedding-3-small) → Store (pgvector)

Runtime: User question → Embed → Top 3 retrieval → Inject into prompt
```

**What to Index:**
- FAQ documents
- Service descriptions
- Pricing tiers (if public)
- Common objections and responses
- Business policies

**Chunking:** 500 tokens with 50-token overlap
**Retrieval:** Top 3 chunks, similarity threshold >0.75

### Guardrails
| Guardrail | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **No pricing commitment** | System prompt: "Never quote exact prices. Offer to have team confirm." |
| **No legal/medical advice** | System prompt: "You cannot provide legal/medical advice." |
| **Off-topic detection** | If irrelevant, redirect: "I'm here to help with [service]. For other questions..." |
| **Competitor mentions** | Neutral response, redirect to value prop |
| **Profanity/abuse** | Polite disengage: "I want to make sure you get the help you need. Let me connect you with our team." |
| **PII handling** | Collect only necessary data, immediate CRM sync, don't store in logs |

### Human Oversight
| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Lead requests human | Instant notification + escalation |
| Confidence <70% | Flag for review, don't auto-respond |
| High-value lead detected | SMS alert to client |
| Conversation >15 messages | Auto-escalate to human |
| Negative sentiment detected | Alert + human review queue |

### Error Handling
| Error Type | Response |
|------------|----------|
| LLM timeout | Retry with exponential backoff (3 attempts) |
| LLM failure | Fallback to backup LLM (GPT-4o) |
| Integration failure | Queue message, retry in 5 minutes, alert if 3 failures |
| Rate limit | Queue and batch process |
| Unknown error | Graceful message: "Let me connect you with our team directly." + alert |

### Cost Optimization
1. **Model routing:** Use Haiku for simple responses, Sonnet for qualification
2. **Prompt caching:** Cache system prompts (saves 20–40%)
3. **Response streaming:** Better UX, same cost
4. **Batch embeddings:** Don't embed real-time; queue and batch
5. **Token monitoring:** Set alerts at 80% of per-client budget

## 7️⃣ Security & Compliance

### Data Encryption
| Data State | Method |
|------------|--------|
| In transit | TLS 1.3 (enforced by Railway/Supabase) |
| At rest | AES-256 (Supabase default) |
| API keys | Environment variables (never in code) |
| Backups | Encrypted (Supabase managed) |

### Access Control
| Resource | Control |
|----------|---------|
| Production database | IP whitelist + strong passwords |
| Admin dashboard | 2FA required |
| Client portal | Per-client auth (Supabase Auth) |
| LLM API keys | Separate keys per client (revocable) |
| Third-party integrations | OAuth where possible, scoped API keys otherwise |

### Secrets Management
**Tool:** Doppler (Free tier) or Railway's built-in secrets

**Structure:**
```
Production/
├── ANTHROPIC_API_KEY
├── TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID
├── TWILIO_AUTH_TOKEN
├── SUPABASE_URL
├── SUPABASE_SERVICE_KEY
├── CLIENT_{ID}_CRM_KEY
└── CLIENT_{ID}_WEBHOOK_SECRET
```

**Rules:**
- Never commit secrets to git
- Rotate API keys quarterly
- Per-client keys where possible (blast radius control)

### Backup Plan
| Data | Backup Frequency | Retention | Recovery |
|------|------------------|-----------|----------|
| Database | Daily (Supabase auto) | 7 days | Point-in-time recovery |
| Knowledge bases | On-change | 30 days | Re-ingest from source docs |
| Conversation logs | Continuous | 90 days | Supabase query |
| Configuration | Git version control | Permanent | Git checkout |

### Client Data Separation
**Architecture:** Multi-tenant with logical separation

| Mechanism | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Database | All tables include `client_id` column, row-level security |
| Vector DB | Separate namespace per client |
| API keys | Client-specific keys for third-party services |
| Logging | Client ID tagged on all logs |
| File storage | Client-prefixed buckets |

**Query Pattern:**
```sql
-- Row Level Security (RLS) on all tables
CREATE POLICY client_isolation ON conversations
  USING (client_id = current_setting('app.current_client_id'));
```

### GDPR Readiness Basics
| Requirement | Implementation |
|-------------|----------------|
| Data inventory | Documented data map per client |
| Consent | Client responsible for end-user consent |
| Right to access | Export endpoint for conversation history |
| Right to deletion | Deletion endpoint + 30-day hard delete |
| Data processing agreement | Template DPA in client contract |
| Data residency | Supabase region selection (EU available) |

### Logging Requirements
**What to Log:**
- All API requests/responses (sanitized)
- LLM inputs/outputs (with PII masking)
- Error events with stack traces
- User actions in admin portal
- Integration sync events

**What NOT to Log:**
- Full API keys
- Raw passwords
- Credit card numbers
- SSNs or government IDs

**Retention:** 90 days (configurable per client)

### Discovery Call Structure (30 Minutes)
```
[0–5 min] Rapport + Context
- "Tell me about [Company] and your role"
- "What made you open to this conversation?"

[5–15 min] Pain Excavation
- "Walk me through what happens when a lead comes in today"

[15–20 min] Vision + Fit
- "If this problem was solved, what would that mean for the business?"
- "What would success look like in 90 days?"

[20–25 min] Solution Overview
- Brief walkthrough of your system (screen share if needed)
- Relevant case study or example

[25–30 min] Next Steps
- "Based on what you've shared, I think I can help. Here's what I suggest..."
- Propose package, quote price
- Schedule follow-up or close on call
```

### Proposal Template Structure
**Format:** Google Doc or Notion (not PDF — feels outdated)

**Sections:**
1. **Executive Summary** (1 paragraph) — What we heard, what we'll solve
2. **Current State** — Pain points discussed (their words)
3. **Solution Overview** — What we're implementing
4. **Deliverables** — Bullet list of exactly what they get
5. **Timeline** — 2-week implementation milestones
6. **Investment** — Setup fee + monthly retainer (no hourly)
7. **Expected Results** — Conservative ROI projection
8. **Next Steps** — Sign, pay deposit, kick off

**Length:** 2–3 pages max

### Closing Strategy
- **Present price verbally first** — Gauge reaction before sending proposal
- **Two options max** — Growth vs. Scale (never three)
- **Anchor high** — "Most clients at your stage do Scale, but Growth might work if..."
- **Time-bound offer** — "This pricing is valid for 7 days"
- **Deposit to start** — 50% setup or full setup before work begins
- **No negotiation on retainer** — "The monthly is fixed; I can adjust setup timing if needed"

## 🔟 Operational Discipline Rules

### What to Refuse
| Request | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "Can you also manage our ads?" | "Not my expertise. I can recommend someone." |
| "We need a custom AI model" | "My system uses leading LLMs optimized for this use case. Custom models add 6+ months and 10x cost." |
| "Can you just do a one-month trial?" | "The system needs 30 days to optimize. Minimum engagement is 3 months." |
| "Our budget is $1,000/month" | "I have partners who work at that level. Let me connect you." |
| "Can you build us a mobile app?" | "Outside my scope. Here's a referral." |
| "We want to own the code" | "Source code is not included. You own your data and configurations." |

### Scope Control Framework

**At Contract:**
- Detailed deliverables list in proposal
- Explicit exclusions section
- Change request clause: "Additional requests quoted separately"
- Revision limits: 3 rounds included

**During Project:**
- All requests via designated channel (email or portal)
- New request? "Happy to scope that — I'll send a quote by EOD"
- "Quick question" that isn't? "Let's add that to our next call"

**Scope Creep Response:**
```
"I want to make sure you get the best result here. That request 
is outside our current scope, which was [X]. I can either:

A) Quote it as an add-on project
B) Add it to our list for Phase 2

Which works better for you?"
```

### How to Avoid Custom One-Off Work

1. **Template-first mentality** — Every client gets the template, then customization
2. **Productized packages** — No custom scoping; they pick a package
3. **If it takes >4 hours, it's a new project** — Quote separately
4. **Document common requests** — If 3 clients ask, build it into the template
5. **No hourly work** — Fixed scope, fixed price, always

### How to Protect Margins

| Threat | Defense |
|--------|---------|
| Client wants lower price | "This is my rate. I can adjust scope if needed." |
| Feature creep | "That's Phase 2. Let's nail Phase 1 first." |
| Over-communication | Weekly async updates + monthly call (not daily check-ins) |
| Problem clients | Fire early. Refund setup, end retainer with 30-day notice. |
| Underpriced deal | Never renew at old rate. Raise at renewal. |

**Margin Protection Rules:**
- No discounts over 15%
- No pilot without payment
- No work before contract + deposit
- No unlimited revisions
- No scope additions without quote

### How to Prevent Burnout

| Trigger | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Too many clients | Hard cap: 10 clients max solo (8 ideal) |
| Reactive mode | Batch communication: Check messages 2x/day, not constantly |
| No boundaries | No weekend work. No same-day responses required. |
| Context switching | Themed days: M/W delivery, T/Th sales, F admin |
| Perfectionism | "Good enough" ships. Perfect never does. |
| No help | Hire fractional help at 5 clients (VA for admin, $500/month) |

**Non-Negotiables:**
- One full day off per week (no exceptions)
- Exercise 3x/week
- 8-hour max workdays (5–6 ideal)
- Vacation: 1 week every quarter
